How It Works

What the detector
is actually doing.

AI Slop Detector does not rely on a single shortcut. It blends a transformer model, phrase-pattern heuristics, sentence-level scoring, and short-text reliability logic to estimate whether a sample reads more like AI-generated prose or human writing.

The short version

The model does the heavy lifting, heuristics provide a secondary signal, sentence-level passes make the output more explainable, and short samples are deliberately softened because tiny passages are too noisy for a strong verdict. Results should be treated as a signal, not proof.

Scoring Pipeline

Every input goes through the same core pipeline. The text is normalized, split into chunks for long passages, scored by a RoBERTa detector, checked for recurring AI-style phrases, and then blended into a final AI-vs-human score.

Primary Signal
82%

For longer passages, the transformer model dominates the blend because it captures broader language patterns better than surface-level rules.

Secondary Signal
18%

Phrase heuristics stay intentionally lighter. They are useful for explainability and nudging borderline samples, but they should not overpower the model.

long-text blend = 0.82 × model score + 0.18 × heuristic score
Signal Blend
The model is the main classifier. Heuristics remain a smaller but interpretable adjustment layer.
Explainability

The app surfaces more than a raw number. It also scores individual sentences and tracks phrase matches so the result is easier to inspect.

ML
Sentence signals show which lines skewed most strongly toward AI according to the model. This helps explain why a sample leaned one way.
P
Phrase hit breakdowns list tracked wording patterns like “furthermore” or “it’s important to note,” along with how much they contributed.
R
Reliability messaging warns when short samples are too weak for a strong conclusion, instead of hiding that uncertainty.
Reliability by Word Count
Below 50 words, the detector explicitly treats the result as weak evidence. Reliability improves as sample size grows.
Short Text Handling

Very short text is the hardest case for AI detection. A 20-word sample simply does not contain enough structure for a robust verdict, so the detector compresses those scores back toward neutral and may mark them as low reliability.

very short text → baseline toward neutral + low trust in score swing
20
20-word minimum keeps the app from pretending that tiny samples are trustworthy.
50+
50+ words recommended because the model and heuristics have enough material to become directionally useful.
90+
90+ words generally gives the detector its most stable and interpretable behavior.
Verdict Confidence Curve
Confidence grows with sample length, but short texts are intentionally flattened so they do not look more certain than they are.
Limits

No detector can prove authorship. Heavily edited AI writing can look human. Highly structured human writing can sometimes look model-like. Scraped text can also be noisy if the page extractor pulls boilerplate or navigation text.

!
False positives are possible. Short, repetitive, or formulaic human writing can look suspicious to the model.
AI
False negatives are possible. Edited AI text can evade phrase rules and look less obviously synthetic.
